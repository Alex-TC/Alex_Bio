A

A (indefinite article): Used before a word or phrase that indicates a type or class of person or thing; often used before noncount nouns that are modified by an adjective or phrase.

Abandon (verb): To stop doing or having (something); to give up (something) completely.

Abbreviation (noun): A short way of writing or printing a word, such as Dr.Doctor or Jan. for January.

Academic (adjective): Usually used before a noun; of or relating to schools and education.

Accept (verb): To agree to (something); to agree to receive or allow (something).

Accurate (adjective): Free from mistakes or errors.

B

Baby (noun): A very young child.

Backward (adjective): Toward the back; to or toward what is behind.

Bacterium (noun): Any one of a group of very small living things that often cause disease — usually plural.

Bad (adjective): Low or poor in quality; not correct or proper.

Bag (noun): A container made of thin material (such as paper, plastic, or cloth) that opens at the top and is used for holding or carrying things.

Bake (verb): To make (food, such as bread and cake) by preparing a dough, batter, etc., and cooking it in an oven using dry heat.

Bank (noun): A business where people keep their money, borrow money, etc., or the building where such a business operates.

C

Cab (noun): The place in an engine or lorry where the drive sits. The same word also means a taxi.

Cabin (noun): A small house or hut made of logs or other rough materials. The same word also means a room for passengers on a ship or aeroplane.

Cable (noun): A very strong thick rope, sometimes made of pieces of wire twisted together.

Cactus (noun): A plant with thick leaves and stems, often covered with prickles, which grows in hot dry countries. You can also grow small ones as house plants.

Calculate (verb): To adjust, estimate, arrange.

Calf (noun): A young cow or bull. The same word also means the thick part of the back or your leg below the knee.

Call (verb): To shout or cry out. The same word also means to stop at someone’s house for a short time.

Camouflage (verb): To disguise something so that it is hidden from the enemy.

Candle (noun): A rounded stick of wax with a wick through the middle. It burns and gives light.

Cane (noun): The hard stem of plant or small palm tree. The same word also means a light walking stick.

D

Daffodil (noun): A yellow spring flower shaped rather like a trumpet.

Dagger (noun): A short sword.

Daily (adverb): Every day.

Dainty (adjective): Pretty;delicate.

Dairy (noun): A place where milk, butter and cheese are kept.

Dam (noun): A special kind of wall which checks a flow of water.

Damage (noun): Harm or injury.

Damp (adjective): Slightly wet.

Dance (verb): To move in time to music.

Dangerous (adjective): Not safe.

E

Eager (adverb): Wanting very much to do something; keen.

Eagle (noun): A large bird of prey, with a sharp curved beak and claws.

Ear (noun): The part of the body you hear with. The same word means a spike of grain such as an ear of corn or barley.

Earl (noun): A nobleman of high rank.

Earn (verb): To get something, usually money, in return for working; to deserve.

East (noun): The direction in which the sun rises; the opposite direction two west.

Eat (verb): To chew and swallow food.

F

Fable (noun): A short story, usually about animals, which is meant to teach us lesson.

Fabric (noun): Woven stuff, frame, building structure.

Facade (noun): Outward appearance, front of building.

G

Gabble (verb): To speak so quickly that people find it hard to understand what you are saying.

Gain (verb): To earn or win; to add to what you already have.

Gale (noun): A strong wind.

H

Habit (noun): Something you do regularly and often, almost without thinking about it, such as brushing your teeth.

Habitation (noun): Residence, abode, occupancy.

Hackney (noun): A horse kept for hire, a coach kept for hire

I

Ice (noun): Water which has been frozen hard by the cold.

Ice cream (noun): A frozen food made with milk and sugar.

Idea (noun): A plan which you think of, or a picture in your mind.

Kangaroo (noun): An animal which can jump a long way. It has a pocket for its babies.

Keep (verb): To hold on to something and not give it away. The same word also means the strongest, inside part of castle.

Kennel (noun): A small house or shelter for a dog.

L

Label (noun): A small piece of paper of cardboard with writing or printing on it. You stick labels on such things as jars, boxes or luggage, so that you know what is inside.

Laboratory (noun): A room or building where scientific tests are carried out.

Labor (noun): Hard work.

M

Macabre (adjective): Terrible, gruesome.

Machine (noun): An instrument, usually made of metal, which does a job of work, like a sewing machine or a washing machine.

Mad (adjective): Crazy; not right tin the head. Someone who is mad does not think properly, because his mind is ill. The same word also means angry.

N

Nab (verb): To catch or seize suddenly or unexpectedly.

Nag (verb): To keep on scolding or finding fault.

Nail (noun): The hard part at the end of a finger or toe. The same word also means a thin sharp piece of metal used to join pieces of wood.

O

O’clock (noun): The time by the clock.

O.K. (noun): All right.

Oak (noun): A kind of tree that can grow very big and lives to a very old age. It has acorns as its fruit.

P

Pace (noun): A step or the length of a step, as when you say something is six paces away, speed.

Pacify (noun): To console, embalm, appease, give peace to.

Pack (verb): To put things into a container. You pack clothes into a trunk or suitcase when you go away. The same word also means a bundle.

Q

Quack (noun): The noise a duck makes.

Quail (noun): A wild bird like a small partridge. The same word also means to lose courage.

Quaint (adjective): Old-fashioned; a little odd.

R

Rabbit (noun): A small furry animal with long ears. Some rabbits are kept in hutches as pets, but wild rabbits dig holes in the ground to live in.

Race (verb/noun): To move very quickly to get to a place before someone else does. The same word also means a large group of people having a similar appearance, especially the same skin colour. Europeans, Africans and Chines all belong to different races.

Radiance (noun): Brightness shooting in rays, splendour, brilliancy.

S

Sack (noun): A large bag made of cloth, paper or plastic.

Sacred (adjective): Holy.

Sad (adjective): Not happy; feeling sorry.

T

Tab (noun): A small flap or loop, usually on a piece of clothing.

Table (noun): A piece of furniture with legs and a flat top. The same word also means a set of facts or figures arranged in columns.

Tack (noun): A short nail with a wide flat head. The same word also means to sew something together with long loose stitches.

U

Ubiquitous (adjective): Existing everywhere, omnipresent.

Ugly (adjective): Not pretty or pleasant to look at.

Ulcer (noun): An open sore on the skin or inside the body.

Vacation (noun): A holidy.

Vaccinate (verb): To give an injection that will prevent you from getting some diseases.

W

Wad (noun): A bundle of paper, often used for packing.

Waddle (verb): To walk with short steps, rocking from side to side, as a duck does.

Wafer (noun): A very thin biscuit.

X

X-ray (noun): A special kind of photograph which shows doctors what the inside of your body looks like.

Xylography (noun): Wood engraving, a process of decorative painting on wood.

Xylophone (noun): A set of narrow pieces of wood that make musical sounds when they are hit with wooden hammers.

Y

Yacht (noun): A kind of boat, usually with sails, used for racing or for pleasure.

Yak (noun): A long-haired ox.

Yap (verb): To bark sharply

Z

Zebra (noun): A wild animal like a small horse with stripes.

Zinc (noun): A bluish-white metal.
